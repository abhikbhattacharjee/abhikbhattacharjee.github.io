Teknothon 2K19



Teknothon ’19 was a 2-day interactive, hands-on workshop held on the 20th and 21st of September, 2019. This year, we chose the theme “Game Design and Development”. In this theme, we expected to familiarise the participants with the basic principles involved in game design process, along with giving them a hands-on feel of developing simple but challenging games. Suitably, the title for this year’s event was chosen to be ‘LEVEL-UP’. Nupur Gujar, Lokesh Chopade, Satyam Jogewar and Nikhil Gupta were invited from Ubisoft as expert guests to conduct the workshop.

With the advent and development of modern GPUs having thousands of processing cores and unparalleled abilities of large number-crunching computations, the field of Computer Games, which was earlier restricted to entertainment and recreational purposes, has now grown into a tech-oriented industrial domain. This domain involves a great deal of logical and computational thinking, and is also a point of attraction for today’s generation. Hence, this year’s theme was centred on the concepts and practices pertaining to the ideation, design and development of computer games.

The first day started with an introductory session by the invited guests. In this module, the participants were acquainted with the very basic fundae of developing games. Then, the participants performed a hands-on exercise in creating a working game of limited scope. During this process, they were guided by the expert guests and learned the nuances in developing an actual gameplay. Finally, the first day concluded with the students having developed some part of a working game, which they would refine the next day.

The second day focused on Game Design, rather than actual game development. This day was dedicated especially to ‘Level Design’. The participants started with the game model which they had developed the earlier day and would refine it much more over the course of the day. Furthermore, the participants were introduced to finer elements of game design, such as User Interface and User Experience (UI/UX) design, and setting up levels for the player. This too was a hands-on workshop on game design practices, conducted under the guidance of the invited guests. The guests had also brought a prize as a surprise to give to whoever brought the most novelty in the design of their game level.

As per tradition, the second day also included Reverse Interview. We had invited guests having a rich experience from renowned organisations. They were: 1.
