Linuxication 2020



In keeping with our motto of sharing knowledge and bridging gaps we at MCUG have been organizing an annual 3-day workshop – ‘LINUXICATION’ for almost a decade now. Every year students of various branches of engineering from colleges all over Pune join us for this workshop. The main aim is to provide the students an insight into the Linux operating system and its vast usage in the industry. We try to encourage students to become a part of the open source community and explore it’s vast dimensions. Various sessions on topics ranging from installation of the Linux operating system, basic commands used in Linux, shell scripting etc. to general open source concepts are conducted. All these sessions are conducted by the members of MCUG, thus providing the participants a friendly atmosphere to learn in. The story of Linuxication so far has been one of immense success. We have received appreciation from all the participants so far and we hope to make it a bigger success in the years to come.




Linuxication 2K19



Abiding by the moto “Sharing knowledge bridging gaps” MCUG(MIT Computer User’s Group) organized their flagship event “LINUXICATION”, a workshop that acquaints budding computer enthusiasts with the basics of Linux based operating systems and provides motivational guidance for being a part of the opensource community. This year the workshop was conducted over three days from 22 nd March to 24 th March in The Department of Computer Engineering at MIT,Pune. The event saw a great response with about 180 students for various colleges in Pune taking part.

The event saw an array of sessions being delivered by the members of MCUG. The first session was that of Installation of the Linux OS, which began by giving the students a brief idea of the firmware basics and the booting process. This was followed by the process of installation where all the students installed Ubuntu flavor of Linux on their laptops. Just to get a hang of the things and make people familiar with the operating system some basic sessions like Directory Structure and Basic Commands followed the installation. The highlight of the first day, however, was the session on Open Source Concepts which gave the students a fair idea about the open source community, the development going on and how they can contribute. To deliver the session people with the industrial experience in the above mentioned domain were invited.

The second day saw some interesting sessions which focused on displaying the true power of Linux and open source softwares. Sessions like Vim, Shell Scripting, Make and many more put forth some of the core fundamentals of automation and programming. The sessions on SSH and remote desktop were also delivered .

The last day was aimed at providing the students with the knowledge of some advanced Open Source technologies. This day consisted of some of the most important and extended sessions like Package Managers, Git, Wine and Compilers. This gave the students a perspective of how Linux is used in industries. The event was concluded by an exciting quiz. Based on the knowledge gained by the students in this workshop, they took part in this final activity with great enthusiasm. The winner of the quiz was rewarded by returning back the participation fee .Lastly, all the participants were presented with their certificate of participation.

With this the last day concluded on a high note with an overwhelming response from the participants who showed an interest in making a shift from windows to the open source softwares. We hope for a bigger event next year!
